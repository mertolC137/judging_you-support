Quick start

The first days can feel confusing because patterns need repeat data. Your charts become much more meaningful after a couple of weeks.

  • Create a few categories you care about (Fitness, Diet, Sleep…)
  • Log small events daily (good or bad—both matter)
  • Review Overview weekly to see trends and momentum
How the scoring works

You define categories (Fitness, Diet, Sleep…). Each log entry adds points to a category. Positive points support your goal, negative points work against it. Over time, the app turns those daily results into trends, comparisons, streaks, and reports.

Important: Keep your scale consistent. If fast food is −2, a great healthy meal might be +1 (not +10). You’re tracking momentum, not “perfect math.”

Understanding the Analytics section

Tap each item to expand.

1. Overview
Your dashboard: trend, momentum, and top contributors

Use this page to understand the big picture. Daily values can bounce up and down; the smoother trend shows direction.

  • Focus on the trend (drifting up or down)
  • Compare daily spikes vs the smoother line
  • Check which categories contribute most to recent performance
2. Insights
Highlights and patterns that repeat

Insights help you spot what tends to lead to good or bad periods, especially after you have enough history.

  • Repeated good days vs repeated problem days
  • Signals before dips (sleep, weekends, workload)
  • Your best “levers”: small actions with big impact
3. Category Deep Dive
Zoom into one category and see drivers

This view is for improving one category at a time. It shows what moves that category most.

  • Which events drive the category the most
  • Whether progress is consistent or “bursty”
  • Gaps: days with few logs can look like flat days
4. Weekly / Monthly Reports
Progress summarized in clean time blocks

Reports reduce noise. They’re best for checking whether you’re improving over time.

  • Week-over-week or month-over-month improvement
  • Best/worst categories for the period
  • Whether you’re stable, improving, or fluctuating
5. Calendar
Timeline view of your days and clusters

The calendar helps you see patterns in context (weekends, travel, deadlines).

  • Clusters: dips often appear as blocks
  • Context cues: weekends vs weekdays
  • Days with fewer logs (useful to notice gaps)
6. Streaks
Consistency tracking for “good” performance

Streaks reward consistency. They’re great for building long-term behavior momentum.

  • Which categories you’re consistent in
  • Where streaks break most often
  • Whether your goal is “do more” or “avoid” (based on category settings)
